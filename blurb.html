PhD in Statistics

Josh has a PhD in Statistical Applied Mathematics at the University of Bath, looking at developing efficient statistical tools for problems arising in spatio-temproal modelling. He was supervised by Prof Matt Nunes and Dr Sandipan Roy.

The issue of fitting high-dimension models when analysing spatio-temporal data in applications such as in environmental, ecological, or epidemiological settings is a well known problem. He developed novel methods to leverage contextual spatial information to produce more parsimonious, interpretable models, through using approximate low-rank and sparse representations.

This proceedure could then be deployed as part of the data analysis pipeline in improving model fit, forecasting, and change-point detection. To demostrate the advantages of his approach, he developed R scripts implementing his model, designed experiments and generated reports of the results from comparative simulations as part of his thesis.
